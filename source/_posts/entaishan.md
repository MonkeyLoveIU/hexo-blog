## Departure: Midnight Arrival by Train

With graduation approaching and our thesis defenses finished, my two roommates and I decided to climb a mountain—our goal was Mount Tai.

We took the train at 5 p.m. on May 30, 2024, and arrived at Tai'an Station at 11:30 p.m.

I thought we might not be able to enter at this hour, but luckily a taxi driver took us to the entrance.

Seeing only a few people ahead, I felt reassured.

## Entering the Mountain: Tickets Across Time

After a short walk, we reached the ticket office—it was already past midnight.

There were still a few people in line. After buying the tickets, I vaguely remember the date printed was for the previous day (since it was already after midnight).

It didn’t matter much. We passed the ticket check and officially started climbing at 12:30 a.m.

There were very few people at the foot of the mountain, just a few coming down.

## Midnight Snack: Pancake with Scallions

About halfway up, I bought a pancake with scallions. I’m not sure if it was cold or just poorly made, but it didn’t taste good.

## Reaching the Summit: Slight Disappointment

We walked at a steady pace and reached the summit before sunrise.

Unfortunately, there was no sea of clouds. I just took a few photos and got ready to descend.

No surprises, no excitement—my mood was flat. Maybe my expectations were too high.

## Descent: Reluctant Cable Car Ride

One of my companions was too tired, so we had no choice but to take the cable car down.
